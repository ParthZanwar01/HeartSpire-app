To complete the Congressional App Challenge application (aside from providing the required video link), the form asks for several other pieces of information.  Based on the fields marked with an asterisk on the Full Application page, here’s what you will need:

* **App name:** the title of your app.
* **Programming languages used:** choose all languages you used to build the app (options include Python, C/C++, Java, JavaScript, Swift, Ruby, Objective‑C, C#, PHP).
* **Brief description of what the app does:** up to 400 words describing its functionality.
* **Inspiration:** a brief explanation of what inspired you to create the app (up to 400 words).
* **Technical difficulties faced:** an explanation of challenges encountered during development (up to 400 words).
* **Potential improvements (version 2.0):** describe what you would add or change if you made a second version (up to 400 words).
* **Lessons learned:** what you learned or took away from participating in the challenge (up to 400 words).
* **Where you did most of the coding:** select one or more locations (e.g., at home, at school, library, after‑school program, theCoderSchool, somewhere else).
* **Date of coding completion:** specify when you finished coding your app.
* **Whether the app was created as part of a project for school/coding club/organization:** select Yes or No.

  * If yes, you must provide:

    * Name of the school/organization.
    * Name of the teacher or mentor.
    * Email address of the teacher or mentor.
* **Ambassador referral (optional):** if an App Challenge Ambassador referred you, include their email address.
* **GitHub link (optional):** if you’ve posted your code to GitHub, you can provide the link.
* **Application ready to submit:** check the box indicating that your application is ready; only the primary applicant can submit the final application.

Providing this information (along with the video link you mentioned) will allow you to complete and submit the application successfully.
